The Innoculated Standard

Grown the hard way,
because it is the right way.

Harvested at Peak

Every flush is watched daily and cut by hand at the exact hour of maturity — never before, never after. What reaches your kitchen was growing this morning.

Nothing Unnatural

No pesticides. No chemicals. No exceptions — since our first flush. Pure hardwood substrate, clean water, filtered air, and patience are the entire recipe.

Grown Slow, Small Batch

We are a family farm, not a factory. Each variety is raised in limited weekly batches so nothing is ever rushed, stockpiled, or shipped stale.

Our Story

Born from a moment
of clarity.

In 2020, a single moment of clarity set our founder on an unlikely path: a year buried in mycology texts, a first grow room built by hand, and a conviction that the food we raise should honor the ground it comes from.

Innoculated Farms is still what it was on day one — a small, family-owned farm in Fairfield, New Jersey, twenty miles from Manhattan and a world away from industrial agriculture. We grow rare culinary and functional varieties on pure hardwood substrate, and when each harvest is done, that substrate goes back to the earth through our Sub to Soil program.

We believe the mushroom is nature's quiet masterpiece — food, medicine, and teacher at once. Our work is simply to grow it faithfully, and to share what it knows.

— The Innoculated family

The Living Collection

Five varieties.

Each strain is grown on its own schedule, in its own climate, and cut only when it has earned its name.

Lion's Mane

Hericium erinaceus

Snow-white cascades with a crab-sweet delicacy. Sears into golden “scallops.”

Blue Oyster

Shiitake

Dense umber caps of smoke and forest floor. The backbone of a serious kitchen.

Maitake

Grifola frondosa

Hen-of-the-woods. Feathered fronds that roast to a shattering crackle.

Chestnut

Copper-capped clusters — nutty, firm, and built for the pan.
